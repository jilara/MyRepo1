Masterpieces

Eiffel Tower

Named after Gustave Eiffel because his company designed and built the structure. The locals gave it the nickname of "La dame de fer" which is french for iron lady. This masterpiece was shown off in the 1889 world's fair.

Himeji Castle

An ancient castle complex built on a hill in himeji in the year 1333. It is a great example of Japanese architecture that survived the bombing of World War II

Roman Colosseum

It is an amphitheatre built in Rome under the rule of the Flavian emperors standing 4 stories high. It is still the largest amphitheatre despite its old age. This amphitheatre was mainly used for gladiator battles for the emperors entertainment.

The Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is an ivory-white marble mausoleum on the southern bank of the river Yamuna in the Indian city of Agra commisioned in 1632 by Emperor Shah Jahan of Mughal. It is a tomb for his favorite wife Mumtaz Mahal.
